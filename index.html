Mass shootings in the US:
On the rise and deadlier

By Pooja Chaudhuri

February 19, 2023

On January 21, 2023, a 72-year-old gunman killed 11 people and nine others who were celebrating the Lunar New Year in Monterey Park, California. This was the deadliest shooting the U.S. has witnessed since the mass shooting last year in Uvalde, Texas where 19 children and two teachers were killed.

The number of mass shootings has been rising since 2014 according to Gun Violence Archive (GVA), which complies the data using public records. And the shootings have become deadlier.

In 2022, U.S. saw 648 mass shootings. While there is no single definition of mass shooting, GVA defines it as an incident where four or more people have either been killed or injured. This is more than twice the number of mass shootings that took place in 2014. In the same time period, the number of people killed in mass shootings has also more than doubled.

In modern U.S history, the deadliest mass shooting took place when a lone gunman fired a barrage of bullets at a country music festival in Nevada, Las Vegas. The massacre left 59 people dead and 441 others injured.

If one looks at the state-wise data, the number of shootings are not consistent across the country. In 2015, a study had linked the high rate of shootings to the high number of gun owners and this trend is dependent on state laws.

Other studies have shown a correlation between tighter gun laws and reducation in gun-related violence. However, this may not always be the case. California has one of the most stringent gun laws in the country but the number of mass shootings in the state has remained high. Several other factors can be responsible. California is the most populous U.S. state and rate of mass shootings is is among the lowest when adjusted for population (per 10,000 people).
